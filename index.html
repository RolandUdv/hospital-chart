Orthostatic Hypotension Chart

Definition: ‘a sudden drop in blood pressure when you stand from a seated or prone (lying down) position’

How to perform: Complete a Lying + Standing Blood pressure. Ask the patient to lie down for 5 minutes or more on the bed. Check blood pressure while patient is in supine position. Then stand the patient and check the blood pressure after 1 minute of standing, then after 3 minutes of standing.

How to identify: A decrease in systolic blood pressure of 20mmHg or more, OR a decrease in diastolic blood pressure of 10mmHg or more.

Associated symptoms with Orthostatic Hypotension
  • Light-headedness or dizziness upon standing
  • Blurry vision
  • Weakness
  • Fainting (syncope)
  • Confusion
  • Nausea
  • Chest pain/shoulder pain/neck pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Headaches
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Heart palpitations
  • Falls
